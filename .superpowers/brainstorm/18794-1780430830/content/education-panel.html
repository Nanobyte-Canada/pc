Bull Call Spread

Bullish
Buy a lower-strike call and sell a higher-strike call with the same expiration. You profit when the underlying rises, with both risk and reward capped by the spread width.

When to Use
  • You expect the underlying to rise moderately — not a moonshot
  • You want defined risk instead of buying a naked call
  • Implied volatility is relatively high — the sold call offsets IV cost
  • You have a specific price target for the underlying by expiration
How It Works
LEG 1 — BUY
Buy Call @ Lower Strike
This gives you the right to buy shares at the lower strike price
LEG 2 — SELL
Sell Call @ Higher Strike
This caps your upside but reduces the cost of the long call
Key Characteristics
Max Profit Spread Width − Debit
Max Loss Net Debit Paid
Break-even Lower Strike + Debit
Ideal DTE 30–60 days

Education Content Structure (Standardized for All 7 Strategies)

  • Always visible: Strategy name, outlook tag, one-line description, quick stats (legs, type, max risk, max reward)
  • Learn More (expandable):
    • When to Use — Market outlook scenarios (bullish, bearish, IV high/low, etc.)
    • How It Works — Leg-by-leg explanation with BUY/SELL labels
    • Key Characteristics — Max profit/loss formulas, break-even, ideal DTE
  • Contextual tips (during leg building): Dynamic warnings from existing EducationEngine (short DTE, wide spreads, deep ITM, etc.)
  • Mobile: Learn More collapsed by default to save vertical space; same content when expanded
