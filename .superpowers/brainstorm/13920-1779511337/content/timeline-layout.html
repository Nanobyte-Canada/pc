How should the 90-day timeline be structured?

This is the core visualization — how positions map to time. Consider: you have multiple tickers, each with CSPs and CCs at various expiries across a 90-day window.

A

Horizontal Timeline Grid

Tickers on the Y-axis, dates on the X-axis. Each position is a card/chip placed at its expiry date. Empty cells = gaps where you could open a position. Think of a Gantt chart for options.

Pros

  • Gaps are immediately visible as empty cells
  • Natural left-to-right time flow
  • Easy to click empty cell to open new position

Cons

  • Gets wide with many expiry dates
  • Limited space per position card
  • Multiple positions at same expiry for same ticker need stacking
B

Expiry-Grouped Card List

Vertical list grouped by expiry date (nearest first). Each expiry section shows all positions expiring that date as cards. A summary row per expiry shows total premium, P&L. Empty ticker slots shown as "Add position" buttons within each group.

  • "Add position" less intuitive — which ticker at which expiry?
C

Hybrid: Timeline Header + Grouped Detail

A compact horizontal timeline bar at top showing all positions as colored dots/chips (overview), plus a detailed expiry-grouped list below (detail). The timeline gives you the 90-day coverage view at a glance; the list below gives you the data. Clicking a position in either view highlights it in both.
